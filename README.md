# 2026-winnicki-june
Talk by Mickal Winnicki

## Abstract

The protein design field underwent rapid advances in recent years thanks to the achievements in the deep-learning space. While early protein engineering relied heavily on random selection and slow, physics-based trial and error, the current landscape is driven by powerful generative AI, such as advanced diffusion models (e.g., RFdiffusion) and multimodal protein language models (e.g., ESM3). Today, we can already reliably design highly specific target binders, novel structural assemblies, and even enzymes from scratch. Despite these major past and recent achievements, we still face critical limitations. Looking to the future, overcoming these hurdles will pave the way for sophisticated, programmable nanomachines and targeted therapeutics that extend far beyond the functional limits of natural evolution.
